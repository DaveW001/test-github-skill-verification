# Army Agile Adoption Lessons

## What the Army context teaches Packaged Agile

1. **Agile cannot be pitched as "Process" — it must be pitched as "Speed of Delivery."**
   - *Lesson:* "Time from need to hand of soldier. Who gives a f*** about solicitation to award?" (*PAE C2 HQ Interview (Mr. Manning)*).
   - *Implication:* Drop generic Agile jargon. Speak exclusively in terms of reducing decision latency, gaining "Decision Dominance," and achieving "Commercial-First" tech refreshes.

2. **Matrixed military environments require explicit authority design.**
   - *Lesson:* Agile team coaching is useless if the team reports to two different four-star commands with conflicting mandates. You must explicitly design the governance layer (e.g., OpCon vs. AdCon boundaries) before training the teams.

3. **Transparency requires Executive Air Cover.**
   - *Lesson:* In a culture that defaults to "Watermelon Reporting" (green outside, red inside) due to fear of program cancellation, transparency is actively punished.
   - *Implication:* Do not ask teams to be transparent without first establishing an Executive Action Team (EAT) that formally rewards the reporting of "Red" status.

4. **Legacy Funding dictates Portfolio Agility.**
   - *Lesson:* "Funding is 'POM’d' 2+ years in advance, leaving leaders with 'limited or no ability to legally flex to different tech'" (*Army organizations, staff, Acronyms*).
   - *Implication:* Lean Portfolio Management (LPM) coaching must directly address the statutory and Congressional limitations of the client's funding lines, establishing safe "Trade Space" rules.

5. **Executives hate abstract organizational design.**
   - *Lesson:* Senior defense leaders operate in life-or-death, highly constrained realities. They actively reject abstract "sticky-note exercises" in favor of tactical tabletops.
   - *Implication:* Make every workshop decision-oriented and executable. Structure consulting as funded, time-boxed "Release Packages" with explicit ROI.
