# Army Challenges and Patterns

## 1. Dual-Hatting & Staff Burnout
- **Challenge:** The PAE was stood up without new headcount (TDA growth). Personnel are "dual-hatted," forced to do multiple jobs simultaneously.
- **Evidence:** "That's a lot to ask somebody to do those three jobs... It's not like I have a bench, three or four people deep that I can just backfill" (*PAE C2CC2 Interview (Ms Ferry)*).
- **Pattern/Intervention:** Harden Operational Control (OpCon) and Accept Administrative Control (AdCon) as fixed. Consolidate all tasking inside the PAE "box" to minimize context switching (*2026-03-26 Dave and Tim Discuss Op Model Notes*).

## 2. Permission-Based Culture & "Watermelon" Reporting
- **Challenge:** The organization operates in a punitive environment where the absence of a rule means waiting for explicit authorization. This fear creates "watermelon" reporting (green on the outside, red on the inside).
- **Evidence:** "A culture of 'watermelon' projects... where problems are hidden until they are critical due to fear of position loss or program cancellation" (*Workshop Synthesis for NotebookLM*).
- **Pattern/Intervention:** Implement a Saab/Gripen Daily Escalation Cascade. Create a 90-minute daily stand-up rhythm that escalates unresolved impediments directly to the top boss, formally rewarding leaders who report "Red" status early (*2026-04-17 Leadership Workshop Out brief Notes*).

## 3. Reporting Drag & CPR Madness
- **Challenge:** Manual reporting and slide-deck creation consumes massive capacity, paralyzing value delivery.
- **Evidence:** "The CPR process is a massive time-sink. It’s taking two months of staff work for a single one-hour meeting" (*March 31, 2026 – Daily Sync Notes*).
- **Pattern/Intervention:** Portfolio Command Center & Flow Metrics. Replace PowerPoint with real-time automated telemetry (Flow Velocity, Flow Time, Flow Load) mapped directly from the system of record (*From Project to Product_ The Warfighting Acquisition System*).

## 4. The "Rolex Problem" (Funding Rigidity)
- **Challenge:** Budgets programmed years in advance prevent Agile reallocation. Teams spend money on low-priority items just to hit targets to avoid losing the budget next year.
- **Evidence:** "Funding is 'binned' and 'POM’d' 2+ years in advance, leaving leaders with 'limited or no ability to legally flex to different tech'" (*Army organizations, staff, Acronyms*).
- **Pattern/Intervention:** Empower an Executive MetaScrum (EMS) to manage "Trade Space" at the portfolio capability level, rather than managing isolated programs. 

## 5. Rejection of Abstract Agile Theater
- **Challenge:** Defense executives reject abstract consulting exercises (like sticky-note "From-To" maps).
- **Evidence:** "Welch gave a visibly negative reaction ('not great face') during this exercise... tactical, scenario-based tabletops... will likely land better with Welch than abstract organizational design exercises" (*Leadership Workshop Notes: Welch's Reactions*).
- **Pattern/Intervention:** Structure engagements as timeboxed "Release Packages" and use concrete, tactical tabletop scenarios to map governance changes.
