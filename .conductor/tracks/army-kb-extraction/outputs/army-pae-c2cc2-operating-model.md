# Army PAE C2/CC2 Operating Model

## Core Design: Scrum@Scale (EMS & EAT)
The proposed operating model utilizes a Dual Operating System approach to manage the transition from rigid programs to a capability portfolio.
- **Executive MetaScrum (EMS):** Acts as the Portfolio Product Owner. It defines the vision, aligns priorities, and manages "Trade Space" (funding and resources) (*From Project to Product_ The Warfighting Acquisition System*).
- **Executive Action Team (EAT):** Acts as the Portfolio Scrum Master. It is accountable for removing systemic bureaucratic impediments (e.g., slow security accreditation, contracting regulations) that individual teams cannot resolve (*From Project to Product_ The Warfighting Acquisition System*).

## Decision Boundaries (OpCon vs. AdCon)
- **Hardening OpCon:** To avoid unwinnable bureaucratic battles with legacy four-star commands, the model explicitly accepts Administrative Control (AdCon) as a fixed constraint.
- **The "Box":** "Decision made: AdCon stays as-is and will not change; all structural changes will target OpCon only... consolidating all OpCon communication within the portfolio box — requirements, tasking, prioritization... so orders come from one place only" (*2026-03-26 Dave and Tim Discuss Op Model Notes*).

## Retained Acquisition Authority
Despite the "Warfighting Acquisition System" narrative, the Portfolio Architecture Executive (PAE) does not hold total authority.
- **MDA Retention:** "I retain MDA [Milestone Decision Authority] or Decision Authority (DA) for all new acquisition programs... PAEs, regardless of existing acquisition certifications, will not serve as the MDA/DA" (*Acquisition Authorities Under Portfolio Acquisition Executives .pdf*). 
- **Implication:** The EMS must carefully navigate escalations to the Army Acquisition Executive (AAE) for major program approvals.

## Measurement and Cadence
- **Flow Metrics over CPRs:** The model seeks to replace two-month prep cycles for Capability Portfolio Reviews (CPRs) with real-time "Flow Metrics" (Velocity, Time, Efficiency, Load) tracked via a Portfolio Command Center.
- **Mission Impact:** Success is explicitly redefined from cost/schedule/performance baselines to "Quantifiable improvement in a unit's speed, effectiveness or survivability measured by contrasting performance in a field environment on standard METL tasks" (*Metrics - CPR Terms of Reference.pdf*).
