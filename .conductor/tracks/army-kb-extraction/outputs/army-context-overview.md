# Army Context Overview

The Army C2/CC2 effort is a transformation from legacy program-centric acquisition (the Defense Acquisition System or DAS) toward a capability-portfolio model (the Warfighting Acquisition System or WAS). The objective is not simply to "do Agile"—it is to achieve "Decision Dominance" by speeding up warfighting capability delivery in a highly matrixed, politically sensitive, funding-constrained environment.

## 1. Mission Context
- **Decision Dominance:** The primary objective of the PAE C2/CC2 is to achieve "Decision Dominance—the ability to sense, understand, decide, and act faster than adversaries" (*PAE C2 HQ Interview (Mr. Manning)*).
- **Speed over Compliance:** The mandate is to "deliver capability to the army faster than we ever have before, outside of the bounds of all the bureaucracy" (*PAE C2CC2 Interview (Ms Ferry)*).

## 2. Operating Structure
- **PAE Matrix:** The PAE is highly matrixed; personnel receive Operational Control (OpCon) from the PAE but remain under Administrative Control (AdCon) of their home units (*2026-03-26 Dave and Tim Discuss Op Model Notes*).
- **Dual-Reporting Friction:** PAEs and Capability Portfolio Executives (CPEs) often have to report to both T2COM (for requirements) and ASA(ALT) (for acquisition), which creates a "bureaucratic burden Manning calls 'bullshit'" (*PAE C2 HQ Interview (Mr. Manning)*).

## 3. Structural Constraints
- **Zero Growth Mandate:** "The PAE is operating under a 'Zero Growth Mandate,' meaning capacity for new initiatives must be recovered by stopping legacy or low-value work" (*Workshop Synthesis for NotebookLM*).
- **Funding Rigidity:** Congress rejected proposals to "bin" money into broad capability portfolios. Funding is "POM’d 2+ years in advance, leaving leaders with limited or no ability to legally flex to different tech" (*PAE C2CC2 Interview (BG Taylor)*).

## Packaged Agile Takeaway
For future Army work, lead with concrete operating-model design, explicit authority boundaries, and measurable flow improvements. Avoid generic Agile language. As Mr. Welch noted during a workshop, senior leaders have a "visibly negative reaction" to abstract organizational design exercises and prefer "tactical, scenario-based tabletops" (*Leadership Workshop Notes: Welch's Reactions*).
